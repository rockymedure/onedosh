# OneDosh — User Categories & Jobs

> Who's doing what, and what job they hire OneDosh to do. Segmented by **position in the money flow**, not demographics.

---

## The four roles

| Role | Who's doing what | Direction of money |
|---|---|---|
| **Earners / Receivers (inbound)** | Nigeria-based people paid from abroad — freelancers, remote workers, creators, students | Money **in** |
| **Providers (outbound)** | Diaspora abroad sending home for upkeep, school fees, hospital bills | Money **across** |
| **Local receivers** | Family in Nigeria on the other end of a Provider's send | Money **out** (locally) |
| **Spenders** | Travelers, global professionals using multi-currency wallet + Visa card | Money **in place** |

**Note:** The highest-value user is often **multiple roles at once** — e.g. an Earner who also sends to family and spends on a card. Design for the stack.

---

## The job each role hires OneDosh for

### 1. Earners / Receivers (inbound) — **primary growth wedge today**

**Who:** Young Nigerians especially — creators, gamers, freelancers, students. Paid by clients, platforms (YouTube/TikTok), brand deals, international gigs.

**Surface job:** *When I'm owed money from abroad, help me actually receive it, hold it in dollars, and convert on my own terms.*

**Deeper job:** *Don't let my livelihood get stuck, frozen, or quietly eaten by the naira before I can use it.*

**Where it breaks today:** PayPal won't receive in Nigeria; first big payment is a freeze magnet; holding value is manual; post-IDV home screen gives no direction.

**Leans hardest on:** **Keep value** (hold USD, don't freeze) + **Get it in** (first receive).

---

### 2. Providers (outbound)

**Who:** Diaspora in US (and expanding: Canada, 29 EU countries). Sending home regularly.

**Surface job:** *When my family needs support, help me get money to them fast and cheap.*

**Deeper job:** *Let me know it landed and did what it was meant to — so I can stop worrying.*

**Where it breaks today:** Slow/opaque transfers; CBN naira-only rule shaving value; no confirmation of purpose.

**Leans hardest on:** **Move it** + **Prove it landed**.

**Growth lever (newer):** Cash App funding — first platform to fund from Cash App → naira.

---

### 3. Local receivers

**Who:** Family in Nigeria. Often less tech-savvy; may not have chosen the app.

**Surface job:** *When money arrives for me, help me get it in a form I can actually use here.*

**Deeper job:** *Keep as much value as possible; trust that it's safely mine.*

**Leans hardest on:** **Keep value on arrival** + **Make it usable**.

**Strategic note:** Invisible users — nobody markets to them, but remittance depends on their experience.

---

### 4. Spenders

**Who:** Global professionals, travelers.

**Surface job:** *When I need to pay for something anywhere, let me spend my dollars smoothly.*

**Deeper job:** *Feel global and unrestricted — not second-class or nickel-and-dimed.*

**Leans hardest on:** **Move it** (spend) + **No hidden erosion**.

---

## Send vs receive — which is the "biggest job"?

| Lens | Answer |
|---|---|
| **Current acquisition (200k users, Dec 2025 → Jun 2026)** | **Receive.** Creator payouts, campus/gaming/community ground game, referrals — all Nigeria-side "get paid." |
| **Market volume ($17–23B/yr US↔NG corridor)** | **Send** (diaspora remittance) may be larger in dollars. |
| **Product activation priority** | **Receive.** Fix post-IDV → first dollar in. That's the leaky bucket. |
| **Deeper job (all roles)** | **Preserve value + certainty** — not send *or* receive, but *don't let the system eat it.* |

**One sentence:** Receiving is the wedge; sending is the scale; keeping value whole is the real job.

## Relational money (corrected framing)

Money in this corridor is **relational**, not purely transactional:

- **Family** — obligation, love, school fees, hospital bills (not "clients")
- **Audience / platforms** — creators paid by YouTube, TikTok, brand deals
- **Business relationships** — freelancers invoicing foreign companies

The entity ("Dosh") should understand **people on both ends** of a flow, not just balances.

---

## Hub user (design anchor)

**Profile:** Young earner in Nigeria. Gets paid from abroad (platform + gigs). Sends some home. Spends on card. Holds in USD when possible.

**Why anchor here:** Touches every flow; highest protection stakes; matches OneDosh's growth engine; post-IDV moment is theirs.
